Employer Resource

Trial Day Framework

A structured approach to running a fair, legal, and genuinely useful trial day. Built for small residential and commercial trade businesses in WA.

Legal reminder: A trial day must be paid. Under the Building & Construction General On-Site Award (MA000020), casual employees are entitled to a minimum 4-hour payment for any engagement. There is no such thing as a legal unpaid trial in Australian employment law.
1
Before the Trial Day
2
On the Day — What to Watch

Rate each area mentally during the day. You don't need to write anything down if you don't want to — but having a framework in your head keeps the assessment consistent.

Punctuality & Attitude
  • Did they show up on time?
  • Were they ready to work?
  • Did they ask good questions?
  • How did they respond to direction?
Trade Skills
  • Is their skill level what they said?
  • Do they use tools safely?
  • Do they know what they don't know?
  • Quality of their work
Safety Awareness
  • Wearing correct PPE without being told?
  • Aware of surroundings on site?
  • Following safe work practices?
Team Fit
  • Did they get along with the crew?
  • Did they show initiative?
  • Are they someone you'd want on site daily?
3
End of Day
4
Outcome Options
✅ Offer the role

Call them — don't text an offer. Confirm the role, rate, start date and employment type. Follow up in writing.

🟡 Second trial day

If you're unsure, a second day is fine. Be clear about what you want to see more of. Still must be paid.

❌ Not the right fit

Call them. Be brief and professional: "Thanks for coming in — it's not quite the right fit for us at this stage." No detail needed.

Privacy Policy
Last updated: April 2026
TradePool WA is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, store, and disclose your personal information, in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

1. Who we are

TradePool WA is a talent register service operated in Western Australia. We maintain a curated register of trade professionals and apprentices and connect them with prospective employers in the residential and small commercial construction sector. We are not a labour hire company, staffing agency, or employer. We do not employ candidates or employers listed on this service.

For privacy enquiries, contact us at: hello@tradepoolwa.com.au

2. What personal information we collect

We collect the following personal information from candidates who register with TradePool WA:

  • Full name, phone number, and email address
  • Geographic region within Western Australia
  • Trade, experience level, and work preferences
  • Licences, tickets, and certifications held (e.g. White Card, Working at Heights)
  • Availability and preferred working arrangements
  • A personal statement written by the candidate
  • Reference contact name and relationship
  • Responses to our AI-assisted pre-vet chat interview
  • An AI-generated summary report of the pre-vet conversation

We collect information from employers who access the register, including business name and contact details.

3. How we collect your information

We collect personal information directly from you when you complete our online registration form and AI pre-vet chat. We do not collect personal information from third parties without your knowledge or consent, except where required by law.

4. Why we collect your information

Your personal information is collected for the following purposes:

  • To create and maintain your profile in the TradePool WA candidate register
  • To match you with prospective employers who may be a suitable fit
  • To contact you regarding your registration and potential opportunities
  • To improve and maintain the quality of the register
  • To comply with our legal obligations

We will not use your personal information for any purpose other than those listed above without your consent.

5. Who we share your information with

Your personal information may be shared with:

  • Approved employers who have been granted access to the TradePool WA register. Employers see a candidate profile that includes your first name, trade, experience level, region, tickets held, work preferences, and AI-generated pre-vet summary. Your full name, phone number, and email are only provided to an employer after you have been matched and you have given consent to proceed.
  • Service providers who assist us in operating this website and storing data (e.g. cloud hosting, form processing). These providers are contractually obligated to protect your information.

We will never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party for marketing or commercial purposes.

6. Data storage and security

Your personal information is stored electronically using secure cloud-based services. We take reasonable steps to protect your information from misuse, interference, loss, and unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure.

Whilst we take data security seriously, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but will notify you in the event of a data breach that is likely to result in serious harm, as required by the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under the Privacy Act.

7. How long we keep your information

We retain your personal information for as long as your registration is active. If you request removal of your details, we will delete your information from the active register within 14 days. Some information may be retained in backup systems for up to 90 days after deletion.

8. Your rights

Under the Australian Privacy Principles, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate or outdated information
  • Request deletion of your information from our register
  • Withdraw consent to the use of your information at any time
  • Make a complaint about how we have handled your information

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@tradepoolwa.com.au. We will respond to all requests within 30 days.

9. Complaints

If you believe we have breached the Australian Privacy Principles, you may lodge a complaint with us directly at hello@tradepoolwa.com.au. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au or on 1300 363 992.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date. Continued use of this service after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

TradePool WA, a talent register service operating in Western Australia.

To create and maintain your profile in the TradePool WA candidate register, and to match you with prospective employers in Western Australia who are seeking trade professionals with your skills and availability.
